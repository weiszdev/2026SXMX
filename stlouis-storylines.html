5 Storylines to Watch at The Dome
Story 01

Tomac Reclaims the Red Plate — But Lawrence Isn't Done

Eli Tomac got the red plate back after Hunter Lawrence went down in the whoops at Birmingham. The gap? Just 4 points. Meanwhile Ken Roczen took the Detroit win and is lurking 14 back. This is a three-rider championship fight with 5 rounds remaining and nobody is safe.

Tomac's worst finish of the season came at Birmingham — and he still gained points. Lawrence needs a bounce-back. Roczen needs another win. St. Louis is going to be violent.

#3 Tomac #96 Lawrence #94 Roczen
Story 02

Deegan Can Clinch Tonight — 250 West Title on the Line

Haiden Deegan sits 42 points clear of Max Anstie and 47 over Levi Kitchen. If Deegan wins and Anstie finishes 5th or worse, the 250SX West title is his — tonight, at The Dome, in front of a Showdown crowd.

Last Showdown, Deegan came from 10th to win on the track but a post-race penalty dropped him to 2nd. He wants redemption. This could be his final 250 race before moving to the 450 class for Pro Motocross in May. Go-out-on-top energy.

250W: DEEGAN 42-POINT GAP — CLINCH SCENARIO ACTIVE
#38 Deegan #35 Anstie #47 Kitchen
Story 03

Cole Davies' 3-Win Streak vs. the East/West Showdown Reality Check

Cole Davies has won 3 straight in the 250 East — he's the hottest rider in the building. But tonight he faces the West guys for the first time since the last Showdown, where he inherited the win after Deegan's penalty.

Davies holds a 9-point lead over Hammaker and 14 over Shimoda. This Showdown doesn't count toward division points, but momentum is everything. Can he beat Deegan straight up?

250E: DAVIES 9 PTS OVER HAMMAKER — 3 CONSECUTIVE WINS
#62 Davies #34 Hammaker #30 Shimoda
Story 04

Sexton Returns, Roczen Makes History, and the 450 Mid-Pack is Chaos

Chase Sexton came back from a 3-round injury absence at Birmingham and immediately podiumed — his 47th career 450 podium. Ken Roczen took his 25th career 450 win, becoming only the 11th rider in class history to hit that mark. Malcolm Stewart posted his best finish of 2026.

Cooper Webb hit 150 career 450 starts. The depth in this class is absurd — 5th through 10th is separated by 33 points. One bad night changes everything.

SEXTON BACK ON THE BOX — ROCZEN 25TH CAREER WIN
#4 Sexton #94 Roczen #27 Stewart #2 Webb
Story 05

The Injury List is Getting Long — Who's In, Who's Out

The attrition rate this season is brutal. Aaron Plessinger is OUT. Mike Brown is OUT with a broken collarbone and dislocated wrist from Birmingham. Austin Forkner is OUT. RJ Hampshire has been sidelined since Seattle with a foot injury. Jason Anderson stepped away for personal and medical reasons — indefinitely.

On the flip side: Levi Kitchen and Linville are IN for St. Louis. Every injury reshuffles the grid and opens doors for riders who've been grinding outside the top 10 all year.
